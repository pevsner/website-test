In Taoism the two great forces of life and existence are Yin and Yang.
They have opposing and complimentary properties.
Neither can exist without the other.
Opposites attact and similar repel.
(Think magnets).

Yin is female in quality (-ve'ly charged).
Yang is male in quality (+'ve'ly charged).

All manifestations are unique because of the varying proportions of these two great forces and how they interact.
Yang spins centripetally (inwardly), Yin spins centrafugally (outwardly). They are depicted as:
Yang = solid line, Yin = broken line.

These lines, when grouped, as either trigrams(3) or hexagrams(6), symbolize qualities of energy. These appear in Taoist systems such as the I-Ching and Feng Shui to describe and calculate energy exchanges.
This enables the propotions and manifestations to be described and calculated.
Grouping of these symbols describe the quality and characteristics of manifestation using Trigrams and Hexagrams.
